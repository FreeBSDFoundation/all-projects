# FreeBSD Foundation CRA Update \- February 2026

## Summary

This month the main focus has been getting the project outline and expected outcomes agreed. This has helped us get aligned on what we want the project to achieve. We also defined the main workstreams for the project. These are; *Security, and Vulnerability Handling*, *SBOM Toolchain*, *Public Documentation*, *Community Legislative Engagement*, *Public Project Repository,* and *Communications*. 

Progress on the workstreams included documenting information about attestations and about the likely responsibilities of the FreeBSD Foundation under the CRA. Work on the SBOM toolchain has continued from an initial project last year. 

To improve coordination, we set up a weekly internal meeting that will start in March. This meeting will help the team share updates and make decisions as the workstreams progress.

We also continue to engage with the wider open source community on CRA topics. We regularly attend Open Regulatory Compliance (ORC) meetings and related events such as the ORC Code and Compliance event and FOSDEM. From these, we have captured details of relevant 3rd-party Requests for Input which we will share with the FreeBSD community soon.

Finally, we published a blog post explaining how the FreeBSD Foundation is preparing for the CRA and created a public repository to share our progress on the project.

## Workstream updates

### Security, and vulnerability handling

This is the most complex workstream, so we have been collecting CRA-related questions and open topics. We will bring these to meetings so we can discuss them and decide how to address them.

We have set up a weekly internal meeting starting in March. This meeting will give the team a regular place to share information and make decisions about the CRA workstreams. This is important because the nature of the work is emergent and involves several different areas.

We started documenting information about attestations so that we can better understand what may be required under the CRA. We also began documenting the likely responsibilities of the FreeBSD Foundation under the CRA. This will help us understand what role the Foundation may need to play, and how close we are to meeting these responsibilities already.

We continued to take part in Open Regulatory Compliance (ORC) community meetings, including the Cyber Resilience SIG and discussions on attestations. These meetings help us stay informed and share information with other open source projects working on similar issues.  Alice Sowerby (Project Manager) and Pierre Pronchery (Senior IT-Security Consultant) attended the ORC Code and Compliance event in January and FOSDEM in late January and early February on behalf of the FreeBSD Foundation. These events provided opportunities to discuss CRA topics with other open source communities and organisations.

We also continued looking for additional funding to support the Foundation’s CRA-related work.

### SBOM toolchain

Work on the SBOM toolchain continued in February. This workstream focuses on tools and processes to generate and manage Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) information for FreeBSD.

More details about this work can be found in the technical update here:  
[https://github.com/FreeBSDFoundation/status-updates/blob/main/Tuukka\_Pasanen/2026-02.md](https://github.com/FreeBSDFoundation/status-updates/blob/main/Tuukka_Pasanen/2026-02.md)

### Public documentation

We published a blog post on February 25 titled “Getting Ready for the Cyber Resilience Act”. The post explains the first steps the FreeBSD Foundation is taking to prepare for the CRA and gives the community an overview of the project.

The blog post is available here:  
[https://freebsdfoundation.org/blog/getting-ready-for-the-cyber-resilience-act/](https://freebsdfoundation.org/blog/getting-ready-for-the-cyber-resilience-act/)

### Community legislative engagement

We drafted a list of open requests for input that third-parties have published and which relate to the CRA. Many of these requests are opportunities for FreeBSD community members to provide  feedback or expertise that will help shape the public CRA discourse outside of the FreeBSD community. We plan to publish it soon and we will keep it up to date over time. We will also share new requests through appropriate mailing lists (e.g. Security).

### Public project repo

We created a public project repository to track the CRA work. This repository will help keep the work transparent and make it easier for others to follow progress.

### Communications

Our main communications activity this month was the publication of the blog post described above. This post is part of our effort to keep the community informed about the Foundation’s work on the Cyber Resilience Act.

# FreeBSD Foundation CRA Update \- April 2026

## Summary

April saw continued progress across all CRA-related workstreams, with efforts increasingly moving from exploration into execution and refinement. The Foundation deepened its engagement with both ecosystem and regulatory stakeholders, including engaging with Zephyr and participating in European Commission consultations via the Open Regulatory Compliance Working Group (ORC WG). Internally, work has stabilised, allowing for a reduction in meeting cadence.

The SBOM toolchain is on track to achieve initial end-to-end functionality soon, with a forthcoming blog post to outline progress. Public-facing documentation is also progressing, with draft CRA guidance prepared for the FreeBSD community due to be published soon. While no new legislative consultations were identified for wider community engagement this month, transparency efforts continued through published reports and communications, including contributions to the Q1 FreeBSD status report and social media updates.

## Workstream updates

### Security, and vulnerability handling

During April, the Foundation contributed feedback to an open consultation from the European Commission, reviewing [draft guidance associated with the CRA](https://ec.europa.eu/info/law/better-regulation/have-your-say/initiatives/16959-Draft-Commission-guidance-on-the-Cyber-Resilience-Act_en). This input was coordinated and [submitted](https://ec.europa.eu/info/law/better-regulation/have-your-say/initiatives/16959-Draft-Commission-guidance-on-the-Cyber-Resilience-Act/F33391529_en) through the Open Regulatory Compliance Working Group (ORC WG). Alongside this, ongoing efforts to deepen understanding of the CRA continued through reviewing the regulation and participating actively in ORC WG discussions and broader FOSS ecosystem initiatives.

The Foundation met with leadership at the [Zephyr project](https://zephyrproject.org/) to compare approaches and share insights on CRA readiness, helping to align understanding across projects facing similar regulatory challenges. Preparations are also underway for a “manufacturer roadshow,” which will involve direct engagement with FreeBSD vendors to better understand their strategies for CRA compliance and related concerns. This is a key route to securing additional funding for the FreeBSD project as manufacturers prepare to become CRA compliant.

As the work in this area has matured, weekly project meetings have been reduced to a bi-weekly cadence, reflecting increased stability in the project work and therefore a reduced need for high-bandwidth communication.

### SBOM toolchain

The [scope of the SBOM toolchain](https://github.com/FreeBSDFoundation/all-projects/blob/main/Cyber%20Resilience%20Act%20Readiness/SBOM-toolchain/README.md) project has now been published on GitHub, providing visibility into its goals and structure. We are also planning on extending the scope and we will share more details in due course.

Development has progressed to the point where the toolchain is nearing initial end-to-end functionality. We plan to publish a dedicated blog post about this milestone which will provide an overview of the system, its capabilities, rationale, and future milestones.

In parallel, the Foundation has continued to participate in SBOM-focused community meetings hosted by OpenSSF. These sessions have provided valuable insight into real-world SBOM usage and emerging best practices, helping to inform ongoing development.

### Public documentation

The Foundation has developed draft guides for the FreeBSD community to clarify how the CRA could affect it. These will form part of the permanent content on the FreeBSD Foundation website and will be published soon. 

These guides also outline how community members can engage with both the FreeBSD Foundation and the FreeBSD Project in relation to CRA-related incidents, helping to establish clearer processes and expectations.

### Community legislative engagement

No new legislative consultations were identified this month that could be shared with the FreeBSD community. The Foundation continues to monitor developments but was not aware of any relevant opportunities for broader engagement during this period.

### Public project repo

A monthly [report for March 2026](https://github.com/FreeBSDFoundation/all-projects/blob/main/Cyber%20Resilience%20Act%20Readiness/monthly-updates/2026-03.md) has been added to the public project repository, detailing progress across all workstreams and maintaining transparency around ongoing efforts.

### Communications

An update on CRA-related work was included in the [FreeBSD Q1 status report](https://www.freebsd.org/status/report-2026-01-2026-03/#_cyber_resilience_act_cra_readiness_project), helping to integrate this initiative into broader project communications. In addition, the March monthly project report was shared via social media, continuing our efforts to keep stakeholders informed and engaged.

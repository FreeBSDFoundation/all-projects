# FreeBSD Foundation CRA Update \- May 2026

## Summary

During May 2026, the FreeBSD Foundation continued advancing its Cyber Resilience Act (CRA) preparedness work across multiple areas, including vulnerability handling, SBOM tooling, public documentation, and community engagement. Discussions with downstream vendors and open source industry groups helped clarify expectations around supply chain due diligence, vulnerability reporting, and SBOM usage. The Foundation also expanded its collaboration with the broader security ecosystem through participation in OpenSSF and Open Regulatory Compliance activities, including joining the OpenSSF as an associate member. Progress was also made on the SBOM toolchain, public communications, and community outreach activities planned around BSDCan and EuroBSDcon.

## Workstream updates

### Security, and vulnerability handling

The Foundation held meetings with downstream vendors to better understand their readiness for the CRA and to identify appropriate points of contact for vulnerability reporting. These discussions explored how vendors plan to conduct supply chain due diligence, how they expect to use Software Bill of Materials (SBOMs), and whether they may be able to offer financial support to FreeBSD-related compliance and security work. The Foundation also attended Open Regulatory Compliance and OpenSSF meetings to stay aligned with evolving industry practices and guidance.

In May, the FreeBSD Foundation [joined the OpenSSF](https://openssf.org/press-release/2026/05/21/openssf-notes-quarter-of-growth-with-new-members-added-ai-security-resources-and-growing-community/%20) as an associate member. The Foundation also attended [Open Source Summit North America](https://events.linuxfoundation.org/open-source-summit-north-america/) in Minneapolis to connect with other open source practitioners and discuss the CRA and related security topics.

### SBOM toolchain

The SBOM toolchain is now close to being fully functional end-to-end. A blog post describing the work and demonstrating the workflow is planned once the remaining pieces are completed. The primary outstanding item is approval of the final functionality to be added to the base system through a third-party imported tool, which is still under discussion.

### Public documentation

The Foundation is planning to add a dedicated section to the Foundation website to address CRA-related inquiries and provide a permanent location for guidance for the FreeBSD community and downstream users.

### Community legislative engagement

No new consultations were shared with the FreeBSD community during May 2026\. The Foundation is not currently aware of any new consultations that could have been circulated to the community during this reporting period.

### Public project repo

A monthly [report for April 2026](https://github.com/FreeBSDFoundation/all-projects/blob/main/Cyber%20Resilience%20Act%20Readiness/monthly-updates/2026-04.md) has been added to the public project repository, detailing progress across all workstreams and maintaining transparency around ongoing efforts.

### Communications

The Foundation is hoping to run a day-long workshop at [EuroBSDcon](https://www.eurobsdcon.org) in collaboration with Peter Hansteen from [BSDly](https://bsdly.blogspot.com). In addition, a short CRA project update will be presented during the Foundation talk at the [DevSummit](https://wiki.freebsd.org/DevSummit/202606) colocated with [BSDCan](https://www.bsdcan.org/2026/index.html).

Attendees of BSDCan may also be interested in attending Peter Hansteen’s talk, “[What has (can) the EU Cyber Resilience Act done (do) for you?](https://www.bsdcan.org/2026/timetable/timetable-What-has-can.html)”, which, while not FreeBSD-specific, will provide a useful introduction to the CRA and its broader implications for open source projects.

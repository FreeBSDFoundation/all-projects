# FreeBSD Foundation CRA Update \- March 2026

## Summary

The project is now fully up and running. Our internal weekly meetings started, giving us a clear structure for sharing updates and making decisions across all workstreams.

Our engagement with the wider open source and regulatory community is strong and increasing. We signed the letter written by the Open Source Initiative to the European Commission in support of voluntary security attestations as a source of empowerment for open source communities. We are making substantial contributions to joint feedback on European Commission guidance which is being coordinated by the Eclipse Foundation. We attended the  FOSS Backstage conference where the most up-to-date thinking of the open source community is being developed. We also reviewed and prioritised which working group meetings and conferences to attend so we can focus our efforts where they have the most impact.

The Foundation’s contracted security lead, Pierre Pronchery, has joined the FreeBSD Security Team. This will help align Foundation work more closely with the FreeBSD Project’s security processes. We also began outreach to downstream manufacturers to better understand their CRA plans and how they can support FreeBSD.

Technical work on the SBOM toolchain continued to make good progress, with early testing and improvements to tooling now in place. 

Community documentation and engagement has also advanced with new public FAQs, and communication through mailing lists, the project repository, and social media.

## Workstream updates

### Security, and vulnerability handling

Our internal weekly meetings began this month. These meetings provide a regular space to coordinate work, share updates, and make decisions across the CRA workstreams.

We met with Jordan Maris from the Open Source Initiative to discuss an invitation to co-sign a letter to the European Commission. The letter highlights the value of voluntary security attestations and how they can help create a better balance between open source projects and commercial users.

We reviewed relevant third-party open source working group meetings and decided which ones to attend, distributing attendance among our team. We also started reviewing conferences in the same way, so we can focus on the most useful events and discussions.

Pierre Pronchery, the Foundation’s contracted security lead, was successful in his application to join the FreeBSD Security Team. He will work directly in the team to support the FreeBSD Project’s readiness for the CRA as well as on all other security matters.

We began targeted outreach to downstream manufacturers. The aim is to understand their plans for CRA readiness and ask for their support in helping FreeBSD meet ongoing needs related to vendor obligations.

We worked with the Eclipse Foundation’s ORC WG (Open Regulatory Compliance Working Group) to respond to a European Commission consultation on a new Draft Guidance document. This document will help explain how the CRA should be applied while legal practice is still developing. We noted that some discussions about open source and stewardship were unclear, and it will be important to ensure that the final guidance reflects the role of established foundations.

We also responded to a request from a third party interested in taking on the role of CRA steward instead of the Foundation. This shows strong community interest, and we hope this group will contribute positively to supporting the FreeBSD Project. The Foundation remains firm in its commitment to serve as the FreeBSD Project’s steward under the CRA.

In addition, we attended the FOSS Backstage conference to learn how other foundations and open source projects are preparing for the CRA.

### SBOM toolchain

The SBOM project remains on track and made solid progress during March. Work focused on improving licence handling, strengthening tools, and producing the first set of .pc file patches. 

We also began early testing of automated SBOM creation.

Some integration and upstream work is still in progress, but the main building blocks are now in place. These will support simpler and more reliable SBOM generation in the next phase.

We also started attending SBOM community meetings hosted by OpenSSF. These meetings help us understand how other organisations are using SBOMs and what best practices are emerging.

### Public documentation

We asked for CRA-related questions from the FreeBSD Security mailing list to better understand community concerns and information needs.

We added a [Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)](https://github.com/FreeBSDFoundation/all-projects/tree/main/Cyber%20Resilience%20Act%20Readiness#faqs) section about the CRA to the project repository README. This provides a simple starting point for community members who want to learn more.   
We also created a dedicated contact email address for CRA questions. Community members can contact the Foundation at CRA@freebsdfoundation.org.

### Community legislative engagement

We introduced the CRA project to the FreeBSD Security mailing list to raise awareness and encourage participation.

We also notified relevant FreeBSD mailing lists about [two new open consultations](https://github.com/FreeBSDFoundation/all-projects/blob/main/Cyber%20Resilience%20Act%20Readiness/legislative-engagement/requests-for-input.md) from European bodies (European Commission and the European Union Agency for Cybersecurity). 

We shared links and encouraged community members to review and provide input where possible.

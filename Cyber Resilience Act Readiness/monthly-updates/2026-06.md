# FreeBSD Foundation CRA Update \- June 2026

## Summary

During June, the FreeBSD Foundation worked with the FreeBSD Project's new Core Team to begin defining the Project’s relationship with the Foundation with respect to the Cyber Resilience Act (CRA) for FreeBSD. The Foundation also continued preparations for integrating SBOM tooling and its associated test suite into the FreeBSD base system, an important milestone towards reliable, automated SBOM generation.

The Foundation continued working on its permanent public CRA documentation, prepared a FreeBSD Journal article explaining the practical benefits of SBOMs and CRA readiness, published the May monthly progress report in the public project repository, and shared a new ENISA consultation with the community. The CRA was also a topic of discussion at BSDCan and a full-day CRA workshop for EuroBSDcon is planned in collaboration with Peter Hansteen.

## Workstream updates

### Security, and vulnerability handling

The FreeBSD Foundation worked with the FreeBSD Project's new Core Team to establish shared understanding of the CRA and to define a clear, documented relationship between the Project and the Foundation in relation to CRA responsibilities.

The Foundation also prepared a FreeBSD Journal article, *Come Friendly SBOMs*, which introduces the CRA and explains the SBOM work and related preparations undertaken by this, and related, projects. The article highlights the practical value that the CRA and SBOMs provide to FreeBSD developers and technical users.

[https://freebsdfoundation.org/our-work/journal/browser-based-edition/improving-software-quality/come-friendly-sboms/](https://freebsdfoundation.org/our-work/journal/browser-based-edition/improving-software-quality/come-friendly-sboms/) 

### SBOM toolchain

The Foundation has been working on the final preparations for including the required SBOM tooling in the FreeBSD base system. This work includes integrating the necessary test suite to help ensure the toolchain operates accurately, reliably, and without adversely affecting the base system.

### Public documentation

The Foundation continued developing permanent public CRA information pages and worked with the new Core Team to finalise their content.

### Community legislative engagement

The FreeBSD Foundation shared a new consultation opportunity with the FreeBSD community during May:

* ENISA consultation on the draft *Technical Advisory for the Secure Use of Package Managers*. [https://github.com/FreeBSDFoundation/all-projects/blob/main/Cyber%20Resilience%20Act%20Readiness/legislative-engagement/requests-for-input.md](https://github.com/FreeBSDFoundation/all-projects/blob/main/Cyber%20Resilience%20Act%20Readiness/legislative-engagement/requests-for-input.md) 

### Public project repo

The FreeBSD Foundation published the [May 2026 monthly report](https://github.com/FreeBSDFoundation/all-projects/blob/main/Cyber%20Resilience%20Act%20Readiness/monthly-updates/2026-05.md) in the public project repository, providing an overview of progress across all CRA workstreams.

### Communications

A full-day CRA and SBOM workshop has been accepted at [EuroBSDcon](https://www.eurobsdcon.org), to be delivered by Pierre Pronchery and Alice Sowerby in collaboration with Peter Hansteen from [BSDly](https://bsdly.blogspot.com).

The Foundation also presented a short project update during its talk at the DevSummit co-located with BSDCan.
